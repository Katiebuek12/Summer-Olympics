Badminton is a racquet sport played using racquets to hit a shuttlecock across a net. It can be
played with teams but it is most commonly played as singles and doubles. Each side may only
strike the shuttlecock, a feathered plastic projectile with a tip on the end, once before it passes
over the net. This sport requires player to have aerobic stamina, agility, strength, speed,
precision and good motor coordination. Modern badminton was developed in the mid-19th
century.

Badminton started in the Summer Olympics in 1992 and 63 different nations have competed in
this sport. It is governed by the Badminton World Federation. There are five different events:
men's singles, women's singles, men's doubles, women's doubles, and mixed doubles. China
has the highest number of medals: with 18 gold, 8 silver, 15 bronze, and a total of 41.
