The Summer Olympic games were first held in 1896. It occurs every four years hosted
in different countries. In each sport you have the opportunity to win a gold, silver, or bronze medal.
The medal ceremony began in 1904. 18 different countries have hosted this event, and 207
nations compete. Since the beginning, it increased in the amount of games and competitors.
The only country to have won a gold medal in every event is Great Britain. Each competitor
must qualify in an international event to represent their nation in their sport, and a maximum of
three individual athletes may represent each nation per competition.

The latest Summer Olympic games was held in Rio De Jinerio, Brazil in 2016. This was officially known as XXXI Olympiad, and was held from August 5th to the 21st. The games featured 28 Olympic sports. Rio became the first South American city to ever host the Olympic Games. There were rumors that the 2016 Olympics would be canceled due to Rio's economic crisis and health concerns surrounding the Zika Virus. Five new events were added at these games, skateboarding, karate, surfing, sports climbing, and the return of baseball and softball.
