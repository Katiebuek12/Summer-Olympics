Swimming became a sport in the early 19th century, and became a sport in the Olympics in
1896. The first events were the crawl, which was renamed as freestyle, and breaststroke.
In 1904 backstroke was added. In the 1940s swimmers found that they could go faster in
breaststroke by bringing both their arms forward and above their heads. This was banned in
breaststroke, thus creating butterfly. Butterfly first appeared at the Melbourne Games in 1956.
Women's swimming was added in 1912 at the Stockholm Games.

The Events

  • Freestlye- 50m, 100m, 200m, 400m, 800m(women), 1500m(men)
  • Backstroke- 100m, 200m
  • Breaststroke- 100m, 200m
  • Butterfly- 100m, 200m
  • Idividual Medley/IM- 200m, 400m
  • Relays- 4x100 free(400m), 4x200 free(800m), 4x100 medley(400m)
  • Marathon- 10km
